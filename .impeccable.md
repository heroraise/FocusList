## Design Context

### Users
FocusList serves students and everyday learners who plan tasks, start short focus sessions, and review study momentum from a phone. They need fast capture, calm feedback, and clear progress without decorative noise.

### Brand Personality
Calm, disciplined, encouraging. The interface should feel like a quiet desk: warm enough to keep users motivated, restrained enough to help them return to the task.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a refined light-first Material 3 Android interface with deep teal as the focus color, muted botanical surfaces, and amber as a small reward/accent color. Avoid generic dark neon dashboards, heavy gradients, and over-carded layouts.

### Design Principles
- Make the active focus session the visual anchor.
- Keep task scanning dense but comfortable.
- Use color to communicate priority and progress, not decoration.
- Prefer reusable theme, drawable, and style resources over one-off XML tweaks.
- Preserve accessibility through clear contrast, touch targets, and readable type sizes.
